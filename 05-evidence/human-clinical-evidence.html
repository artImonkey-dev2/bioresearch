Human Clinical Evidence

Эта заметка предназначена для накопления: - RCT - cohort/meta-analysis results - biomarker vs clinical endpoint distinctions - ongoing trial status for senolytics / reprogramming / plasma-based interventions

Verified updates from literature pass 1

Partial reprogramming / ER-100

  • Confirmed trial record: NCT07290244
  • Title: Evaluating ER-100 for Safety in People With Glaucoma or Non-Arteritic Anterior Ischemic Optic Neuropathy (Optic Nerve Conditions)
  • Sponsor: Life Biosciences Inc.
  • Status observed on ClinicalTrials.gov: Recruiting
  • Phase: Phase 1, first-in-human
  • What this means: there is now a verified human trial record for an epigenetic therapy candidate in a localized ophthalmic setting.
  • What it does not mean: no demonstrated anti-aging efficacy in humans yet; the verified objective is safety/tolerability in optic neuropathies, not lifespan extension.

Senolytics in humans

  • Idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis pilot RCT:
  • Senolytics dasatinib and quercetin in idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis: results of a phase I, single-blind, single-center, randomized, placebo-controlled pilot trial on feasibility and tolerability
  • DOI: 10.1016/j.ebiom.2023.104481
  • PMID: 36857968
  • Main take-away: intermittent D+Q appeared feasible and generally well tolerated in a very small pilot sample, but the trial was underpowered for meaningful efficacy conclusions.
  • Mild Alzheimer's / at-risk older adults:
  • Evaluation of exploratory fluid biomarkers from a phase 1 senolytic trial in mild Alzheimer's disease
  • DOI: 10.1016/j.neurot.2025.e00591
  • PMID: 40274471
  • Main take-away: early-stage human work exists, but biomarker and efficacy interpretation remains preliminary.
  • Pilot cognition/mobility study:
  • A pilot study of senolytics to improve cognition and mobility in older adults at risk for Alzheimer's disease
  • DOI: 10.1016/j.ebiom.2025.105612
  • PMID: 40010154
  • Main take-away: feasibility/safety signal and exploratory outcomes, not definitive proof of broad anti-aging benefit.

Bottom-line for human evidence

  • Human senolytic evidence is real but early.
  • Human partial reprogramming evidence is trial-stage and localized, not proof of systemic rejuvenation.
  • Neither area currently justifies strong claims of validated lifespan extension in humans.

Practical interventions: pass 3 update

Exercise

  • Strongest current human signal across function, morbidity, and mortality association.
  • Best-supported practical anti-aging intervention in this vault.
  • PMIDs: 31434697, 35247352, 36854652, 37182530

Caloric restriction

  • Human evidence strongest for biomarkers and cardiometabolic risk factors.
  • Not yet proven as a human lifespan-extending intervention.
  • PMIDs: 31303390, 26187233, 32803412

Omega-3 and vitamin D

  • Broad anti-aging / lifespan claims are weak.
  • Potential benefit is context-dependent and indication-dependent.
  • PMIDs: 31733345, 31405892, 31567003, 32114706

Sleep

  • Strong practical value for function, symptoms, and risk architecture.
  • Direct proof of lifespan extension via generic sleep optimization is lacking.
  • PMIDs: 26140821, 30058510, 34657160
