Animal Lifespan Evidence

Эта заметка предназначена для накопления проверенных результатов по моделям животных: - что именно продлевало lifespan, - в какой модели, - насколько reproducible, - что было побочным эффектом, - какова вероятная переводимость.

Verified updates from literature pass 1

Epigenetic information loss / causality model

  • Loss of epigenetic information as a cause of mammalian aging.
  • DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2022.12.027
  • PMID: 36638792
  • Relevance: landmark mechanistic paper supporting the idea that induced epigenomic disruption can drive aging-like phenotypes in mammals and that some of these changes may be reversible with OSK-mediated interventions.
  • Caveat: strong support for causality in a model system, not final proof that epigenetic drift is the sole root cause of natural human aging.

Later support / liver-focused causal evidence

  • Reversible and causal epigenetic information loss in liver aging and disease.
  • DOI: 10.1093/pnasnexus/pgag109
  • PMID: 42028154
  • Relevance: supports tissue-level reversibility and causal interpretation in liver aging/disease contexts.
  • Caveat: tissue-specific and still not equivalent to whole-organism lifespan proof in humans.

Verified updates from literature pass 2

Partial reprogramming in progeroid mice

  • In Vivo Amelioration of Age-Associated Hallmarks by Partial Reprogramming.
  • DOI: 10.1016/j.cell.2016.11.052
  • PMID: 27984723
  • Main result: short-term cyclic OSKM expression ameliorated hallmarks of aging and prolonged lifespan in a mouse model of premature aging.
  • Interpretation: this is important proof-of-principle, but it is not evidence of large lifespan extension in normal wild-type mice.

Partial reprogramming in very old wild-type mice

  • Gene Therapy-Mediated Partial Reprogramming Extends Lifespan and Reverses Age-Related Changes in Aged Mice.
  • DOI: 10.1089/cell.2023.0072
  • PMID: 38381405
  • Main result from abstract: systemically delivered inducible OSK in 124-week-old male mice extended median remaining lifespan by 109% over controls and improved several health parameters.
  • Interpretation: this is a real and notable late-life intervention result, but the key phrase is remaining lifespan, not total lifespan from birth.
  • Caveat: do not casually reinterpret this as a 109% increase in full lifespan.

Chemical partial reprogramming

  • Chemical reprogramming ameliorates cellular hallmarks of aging and extends lifespan.
  • DOI: 10.1038/s44321-025-00265-9
  • PMID: 40588563
  • Main result from abstract: chemical-induced partial reprogramming improved aging phenotypes in aged human cells and extended lifespan in vivo in model systems.
  • Caveat: requires careful model-by-model reading before using in headline claims.

Reading rule

For this vault, animal evidence can substantially raise confidence in biological plausibility, but it should not be translated into human lifespan claims without explicit human data.
