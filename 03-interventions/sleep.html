Sleep

Summary

Сон — это реальный healthspan lever, но не в том смысле, что у нас есть RCT showing lifespan extension from “sleep optimization”. Сильнее всего здесь выглядят связи с daytime function, metabolic and inflammatory regulation, and morbidity risk; слабее — direct proof on mortality change through intervention.

Human evidence by endpoint

Biomarkers

  • Sleep disturbance and sleep deprivation are linked with inflammatory activation and worse metabolic regulation.
  • Это делает sleep biologically plausible anti-aging target, especially via inflammaging / neuroendocrine stress pathways.

Function

  • Хороший practical signal: better sleep and treatment of insomnia/OSA can improve daytime symptoms, fatigue, perceived function and quality of life.
  • В clinical reality это часто важнее, чем abstract longevity rhetoric.

Morbidity

  • Poor sleep is associated with higher cardiometabolic and neuropsychiatric risk.
  • But for generic “sleep optimization” the evidence often comes from observational associations or disease-specific interventions, not from giant longevity RCTs.

Mortality / lifespan

  • Associations between abnormal sleep duration and all-cause mortality exist, especially for long sleep and, less consistently, short sleep.
  • However, this does not equal proof that fixing sleep in a generic population causally extends lifespan.
  • For hard outcomes, direct interventional evidence remains limited.

What is reasonably established

  • Sleep matters for healthspan, function, mood, fatigue, and likely part of inflammatory/metabolic risk architecture.
  • Treating clear sleep pathology and improving sleep regularity/quality is practical and justified.

What is not established

  • Нет доказанного broad human lifespan-extension effect from generic sleep optimization alone.
  • Нельзя без оговорок превращать sleep-duration epidemiology в causal longevity numbers.

Key supporting evidence

  • PMID: 26140821 — sleep disturbance, sleep duration, and inflammation meta-analysis.
  • PMID: 30058510 — sleep duration and all-cause mortality meta-analysis.
  • PMID: 32723316 — updated dose-response meta-analysis in older adults.
  • PMID: 34657160 — CBT-I improved insomnia, fatigue, and function in people with chronic heart failure and insomnia.
  • PMID: 30529774 — CPAP meta-analysis and hard cardiovascular outcomes caveat in OSA/CVD.

Bottom line for dashboard

  • Biomarkers: low-moderate
  • Function: moderate-high
  • Morbidity: moderate
  • Mortality/lifespan: low direct interventional proof; observational signal exists
