Omega-3 and Vitamin D

Summary

Omega-3 and vitamin D belong in the pragmatic layer only as context-dependent supportive interventions, not as major anti-aging levers. Human evidence here is much weaker than for exercise, and harder outcomes are mixed or often null at the broad population level.

Human evidence by endpoint

Biomarkers

  • Возможны modest improvements in selected inflammatory, triglyceride, deficiency-related or bone-health-related markers depending on baseline status.
  • Эффект сильно зависит от того, есть ли исходный дефицит, low fish intake, elevated triglycerides или иной specific indication.

Function

  • Для general-function / broad anti-aging benefit signal weak.
  • Vitamin D can matter more when deficiency correction is relevant; без дефицита ожидать крупный anti-aging effect не стоит.

Morbidity

  • Omega-3 may show some context-specific cardiovascular benefit, but broad prevention results are mixed.
  • Vitamin D did not clearly reduce the main VITAL primary endpoints for total invasive cancer incidence or major CVD events in the simple “take it for longevity” sense.
  • То есть morbidity benefit is not zero, but it is not a high-confidence general anti-aging effect.

Mortality / lifespan

  • Для broad all-cause mortality / lifespan extension signal weak or unconvincing.
  • Корректнее считать их supportive-risk-modifiers in selected contexts, not lifespan interventions.

What is reasonably established

  • These supplements can be reasonable when tied to deficiency, diet pattern, triglycerides, bone health, or specific cardiovascular context.
  • They do not belong in the same decision tier as exercise or even well-run sleep optimization.

What is not established

  • Нет убедительного основания считать omega-3 + vitamin D general-purpose anti-aging stack with meaningful lifespan extension.
  • Нельзя переносить subgroup or secondary signals на всех подряд.

Key supporting evidence

  • PMID: 31733345 — principal results of VITAL and updated vitamin D meta-analyses.
  • PMID: 31405892 — vitamin D supplementation and mortality meta-analysis.
  • PMID: 31567003 — marine omega-3 supplementation and cardiovascular disease meta-analysis.
  • PMID: 32114706 — Cochrane review of omega-3 for primary and secondary prevention of CVD.
  • PMID: 34612056 — omega-3 supplementation and atrial fibrillation risk in cardiovascular outcome RCTs.

Bottom line for dashboard

  • Biomarkers: low-moderate / context-dependent
  • Function: low
  • Morbidity: low-moderate / indication-dependent
  • Mortality/lifespan: low or unconvincing as a broad longevity intervention
